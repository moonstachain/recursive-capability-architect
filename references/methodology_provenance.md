# Methodology Provenance

## Sources Of The Pattern

This Skill combines established engineering and experimental ideas:

- separation of execution and independent verification;
- preregistration and held-out evaluation;
- fixed-budget comparison;
- champion/challenger governance;
- artifact identity and evidence-backed completion;
- feedback-to-rule distillation and reversible deployment.

Its recursive-improvement framing is informed by public AIDE and AIDE2 materials, especially the distinction between an inner task-solving system and an outer system that proposes and evaluates changes to the inner system.

Public references:

- AIDE: AI-Driven Exploration in the Space of Code, arXiv 2502.13138.
- Weco AI, First Evidence of Recursive Self-Improvement.
- Weco AI, Four Levels of Recursive Self-Improvement.

## Where The Analogy Stops

Business and creative operations are noisier than deterministic code benchmarks:

- outcomes are delayed and partially observed;
- platforms, customers and environments change;
- identical reruns may be unsafe or impossible;
- human values and brand constraints are not reducible to one reward;
- local visibility separation is process isolation, not cryptographic security.

Therefore this Skill does not claim to reproduce AIDE2, guarantee autonomous self-improvement, or prove Level 1 from synthetic tests. It adapts evaluation discipline to governed human-AI operations.

## Claim Discipline

Use `experimental_level_0` until repeated real outcomes show net-positive, fixed-budget, cross-family and held-out improvement. One successful case, a high completion score, or a well-formed architecture is not recursive self-improvement evidence.
